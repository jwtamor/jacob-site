As an Electrical Engineering Intern at Square, I designed a PCB using Altium to monitor the battery power behavior in the 2nd generation Square Reader. The board is connected to the reader and the battery, measures voltage and current output from the battery, and logs that information to be analyzed later. Additionally, the PCB is equipped with a discharging module that to analyze the reader in low power mode. I designed both the schematic and layout of the board, as well as sourced the components for current monitoring, power, and discharging. I also made prototypes and simulations in order to provide a proof of concept for each of the board modules.
My time as an Optical Engineering Intern and Assitant at Calix allowed me to practice software and hardware interaction. During my summer internship there, I fronted the projects for automating the qualification tests for optical equipment. These automation scripts remotely accessed the device under test in order to receive information and run the qualifications. The Vice President of Hardware Engineering eventually evaluated this model and mandated the work on hardware automation. I continued to work throughout the school year where i constructed models and simulations in Python in order to guide the understanding of signal attenuation and its practical consequences.
